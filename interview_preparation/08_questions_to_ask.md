# Smart Questions to Ask the Interviewer

> Asking good questions shows preparation, domain knowledge, and genuine interest.
> **Always have 3-5 questions ready. Never say "I have no questions."**

---

## Category 1: About the Role (Ask First)

### Q1: "Can you tell me more about the specific ECU or system I'd be validating?"
**Why ask**: Shows you think in terms of specific systems, not generic "testing."
**Follow-up**: "Is it a new platform or a carry-over from a previous generation?"

---

### Q2: "What does a typical day or sprint look like for someone in this role?"
**Why ask**: Understand daily workflow — are you executing tests, designing them, or both?
**Follow-up**: "How much of the role is hands-on testing vs. test planning/documentation?"

---

### Q3: "What tools and infrastructure does the team use?"
**Why ask**: Shows you're ready to contribute — you already know CANoe, dSPACE, CAPL, etc.
**Listen for**: If they mention tools you know, you can say: "That's great — I've been using CANoe and CAPL extensively at BYD."

---

### Q4: "Is there an existing automation framework, or would I help build one?"
**Why ask**: Shows your automation mindset — you're not just a manual tester.
**Follow-up**: "At BYD, I built a CAPL-based automation framework that covered 120 test cases — I'd be happy to bring that experience here."

---

## Category 2: About the Team & Process

### Q5: "What's the team size and structure? How does test interact with development?"
**Why ask**: Understand if you'll be embedded with developers or in a separate test team.
**Follow-up**: "Is the team co-located or distributed across geographies?"

---

### Q6: "What testing processes do you follow? Agile, V-Model, or hybrid?"
**Why ask**: Shows process maturity awareness.
**Follow-up**: "How is test traceability managed — do you use DOORS or another requirements tool?"

---

### Q7: "How does the team handle critical bug escalation?"
**Why ask**: Shows you've dealt with critical bugs and understand the escalation process.
**Follow-up**: "At BMW, we had a defined P1 escalation flow — I'd be interested to know your process."

---

## Category 3: About Growth & Learning

### Q8: "What does the career growth path look like for this role?"
**Why ask**: Shows you're thinking long-term, not just about the immediate position.
**Listen for**: Do they have a clear progression (Engineer → Senior → Lead → Architect)?

---

### Q9: "Does the team support certifications or training — like ISTQB, ASPICE, or ISO 26262 courses?"
**Why ask**: Shows continuous learning mindset.
**Follow-up**: "I'm interested in deepening my ISO 26262 functional safety knowledge — would that be supported?"

---

### Q10: "Are there opportunities to work on advanced features like Level 3 autonomy or AUTOSAR-based platforms?"
**Why ask**: Shows ambition and awareness of industry direction.
**Follow-up**: "I've been studying AUTOSAR fundamentals during my recent upskilling — I'd love to apply that knowledge."

---

## Category 4: About the Project / Product

### Q11: "What stage is the project in — concept, development, or production validation?"
**Why ask**: Your contribution differs significantly by phase.
**Follow-up**: "Are you following ASPICE or ISO 26262 processes for qualification?"

---

### Q12: "What are the biggest testing challenges the team is currently facing?"
**Why ask**: This is a POWER QUESTION — it shows you're already thinking about how to help.
**Follow-up**: Listen carefully, then relate to your experience: "That's interesting — at BYD we faced something similar with [X], and we solved it by..."

---

### Q13: "How do you validate sensor performance — is it bench/HIL based or vehicle-level?"
**Why ask**: Shows your understanding of the difference between bench, HIL, and vehicle testing.
**Follow-up**: "I've worked across all three — HIL with dSPACE at BMW/BYD, and vehicle-level at all three OEMs."

---

## Category 5: About Company Culture

### Q14: "How would you describe the team culture — is it more collaborative or independent?"
**Why ask**: Important for your working style. Also shows you care about team fit.

---

### Q15: "What do you enjoy most about working here?"
**Why ask**: Personal question — makes the interviewer reflect positively. Builds rapport.

---

## How to Choose Which Questions to Ask

| Interview Type | Best Questions |
|---------------|---------------|
| **HR / Recruiter** | Q2, Q8, Q14, Q15 |
| **Technical Manager** | Q1, Q3, Q4, Q12, Q13 |
| **Senior Engineer / Peer** | Q1, Q5, Q6, Q7, Q12 |
| **Director / VP** | Q8, Q10, Q11, Q12 |
| **Final Round** | Q8, Q10, Q12, Q15 |

---

## Questions to AVOID

| Don't Ask | Why |
|----------|-----|
| "What does your company do?" | Shows zero research |
| "How much is the salary?" (in round 1) | Premature — wait for HR round or offer stage |
| "Can I work from home every day?" | Focus on the role first, negotiate WFH after offer |
| "When can I get promoted?" | Sounds entitled — instead ask Q8 (growth path) |
| "Did I get the job?" | Just don't. |
| "Nothing — I have no questions" | Worst possible answer — always have 3+ ready |

---

## Pro Tips

1. **Write your top 5 questions on a notepad/phone** — it's okay to glance at them
2. **Adapt based on what was already covered** — don't repeat what the interviewer told you
3. **Use their answer to your question as a conversation** — don't just say "okay, next question"
4. **End with Q15** ("What do you enjoy most?") — it leaves a positive emotional impression
5. **If you genuinely liked the conversation, say so**: "I've really enjoyed this discussion — the project sounds exciting and aligned with my experience."
